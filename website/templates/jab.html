Jab - 1

The most important strike in boxing and arguably all combat sports. Thrown from the lead hand in a straight line, it's the fastest and most direct strike. Its strength lies in its versatility and ability to be used while moving as well as its ability to stop your opponent from moving. The basic jab is executed by extending the lead hand out in a straight line making sure your shoulder comes up to protect the chin, ensuring full extension and hip rotation.
Notable Users: Gennady Golovkin, Vasily Lomanchenko, Jermall Charlo

45-Degree Jab

A powerful jab thrown from a low position, can be executed from a low hand or a crouching position, throwing the jab at an upward angle.
Notable Users: 'Iron' Mike Tyson, Donovan 'Razor' Ruddock, Manny Pacquiao

Back-Hand Jab

A jab thrown where the impact is on the back of the hand or the knuckle of the pinky. Useful after throwing a lead hook or from a low hand position using a whipping motion, sometimes confused for an up jab
Notable Users: ‘Sugar’ Shane Mosely, Muhammad Ali, Roy Jones Jr.

Back-Up Jab

A useful jab for boxers on the move, executed by stepping backward and throwing the jab at the oncoming opponent.
Notable Users: Floyd Mayweather, Muhammad Ali, Larry Holmes

Body Jab

An effective jab aimed at the body, specifically the solar plexus,diaphragm or the pit of the stomach. Used to disrupt the opponent’s breathing, rhythm, and can even break their balance. Can be considered a variant of the stiff/step-in jab specifically since they’re executed the same way, just thrown at different targets.
Notable Users: Floyd Mayweather, Thomas Hearns, Andre Ward

Feint/Fake Jab

A tool used to set up a more powerful punch or disrupt your opponent, executed by only partially extending the lead hand then retracting it.
Notable Users: Tyson Fury, Floyd Maywather, Guillermo Rigondeaux

Flicker Jab

Similar to the pawing jab it has little power but can be used to occupy your opponents vision or guard. Executed by throwing several jabs from the elbow.
PNotable Users: Thomas Hearns, Sugar Shane Mosley, Vasily Lomanchenko

Pawing Jab

While this jab has little power, it’s effectiveness comes from occupying your opponent’s guard and line of sight. Executed by throwing lightly and leaving the arm extended either occupying the area in front of your opponent’s guard or resting on their glove, shoulder or head.
Notable Users: Larry Holmes, Wladimir Klitschko, Tyson Fury

Power Jab

The most powerful jab, with potential to knock an opponent out. Generally the power jab is executed by pushing off the back foot as you jab, putting your whole body weight and momentum behind the punch. Can knock your opponent off balance or split your opponent’s guard which you can then follow through with a straight or cross.
Notable users: Manny Pacquiao, Miguel Cotto, Ike Quartey

Step-Through Jab

The second most powerful jab, the step through jab is executed by taking a deep step in with the rear foot then stepping through with the lead leg and throwing the jab, the power is generated by the momentum of your body going forward and the twisting of your hips as you shift your weight from your right leg to the left.
Notable Users: Sergey Kovalev and Vasily Lomanchenko

Stiff/Step-In Jab

A more powerful jab where the boxer steps in with his lead foot as he jabs and retracts his lead foot and jab at the same time. This can be used to fool your opponent into thinking you have more range and power than you actually possess. Can also be used after fooling your opponent into thinking he’s at a safe distance.
Notable Users: Floyd Mayweather, Saul Canelo Alvarez, Juan Manuel Marquez

Swivel Jab

Another powerful jab, whose power is generated mainly by the hips, it is generally thrown from an open stance or after a cross/straight, where the rear shoulder is closer to the opponent than the lead shoulder. The jab is then thrown to reset into a bladed stance and catch your opponent off guard if they attempt to counter or attack in your vulnerable squared position.
Notable Users: Erislandy Lara, Noaya Inoues

Trip-Hammer Jab

Not a particularly strong jab, thrown from a high guard, used to occupy or remove your opponents guard. Thrown in a downward motion from the high guard.
Notable Users: Adrien Broner, Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez

Up Jab

A jab thrown from a low lead hand, point of impact is the top of the knuckles. Usefulness comes from how hard it is to see coming and can pick up the chin of the opponent.
Notable Users: Floyd Mayweather, James Toney, Tyson Fury

Vertical Jab

A useful jab used to split the guard. Executed like a normal jab without turning the fist over.
Notable Users: Manny Pacquiao, Jack Dempsey, 'Dutch' Sam
